Emotion Variation

These data capture one person's reported emotional experiences over two weeks. Each dot represents a set of emotion instances. Using bodily signals recorded at the time of each report, we clustered each report based on the best fitting physiological pattern (A, B, C, D, E, or unclustered). Larger dots indicate more emotion instances.

CONSISTENCY:

For a given emotion, is there a consistent pattern of physiological change?

Looking just at instances when this person reported feeling happy, there wasn't just one corresponding pattern of physiological change. Instead, this emotion was characterized by many different physiological patterns.

SPECIFICITY:

Do instances of different emotions correspond to specific patterns of physiological change?

Looking just at Pattern A, this person reported feeling many different emotions even when their physiological patterns were similar. This suggests a pattern of physiological changes isn't specific to one emotion.

OVERALL:

What about other emotions and physiological patterns?

Across the full dataset, instances of each emotion don't consistently correspond to a single physiological pattern. Likewise, physiological patterns aren't specific to just one emotion.
